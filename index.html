01 · About

Researcher at the edge of quantum & materials science

Bridging computational physics and nanomaterial engineering to design the next generation of clean-energy storage systems.

I am a materials science researcher focused on hydrogen storage, MOF–aerogel composite materials, adsorption–diffusion dynamics, and quantum tunneling at cryogenic temperatures.

My work combines computational modeling, thermodynamic analysis, and nanomaterial engineering to understand how hydrogen behaves when confined inside porous architectures at temperatures approaching absolute zero — where classical diffusion gives way to quantum mechanical tunneling.

The goal: to design material platforms that can safely, densely, and reversibly store hydrogen for the clean energy systems of the future — from fuel cells to space propulsion and deep-cryogenic transport infrastructure.

05 · Molecular Simulation Library

Live view: H₂ diffusing in a MOF unit cell

A simplified real-time rendering of hydrogen molecules exploring the pore network of a metal–organic framework — the same physics I simulate at cryogenic temperatures.

◉ SIM.RUNNING T = 77 K · P = 1 atm
frame: 0 Δt = 0.5 fs · 3D periodic

Simulation parameters

Each run solves classical/quantum equations of motion for hundreds of H₂ molecules inside a periodic MOF unit cell — capturing adsorption isotherms, self-diffusion coefficients, and tunneling probabilities.

  • Ensemble: NVT / μVT (GCMC) at 77 K
  • Force field: UFF + TraPPE-H₂ custom fit
  • Quantum correction: Feynman–Hibbs effective potential
  • Timesteps: 10⁷ MD · 2×10⁶ GCMC cycles
  • Analysis: MSD, RDF, isosteric heat, transmission coeff.

07 · Materials Showcase

Architecture of the MOF–Aerogel composite

Three structural motifs combine to deliver exceptional hydrogen uptake: the crystalline order of MOFs, the ultralow density of aerogels, and the emergent interface between them.

01 · Framework

MOF Lattice

Periodic metal-node + organic-linker crystal providing tunable pore geometry and high surface area.

02 · Matrix

Silica Aerogel

A nanoporous, 95%+ void network providing mechanical support and macro-scale mass transport.

03 · Hybrid

Composite Interface

MOF crystals nucleated inside aerogel pores — combining crystallinity and hierarchical porosity.
